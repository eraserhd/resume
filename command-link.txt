About Your New Role

We’re seeking an exceptional Technical Leader to spearhead the next evolution of our 13-year-strong Quote Engine—a mission-critical system powering our quoting and pricing operations. As the Founding Engineer for this transformation, you’ll lead the architectural reimagining of a complex, high-throughput system and play a pivotal role in shaping the product and technical roadmap for the next decade.

This is a rare opportunity to build the future while standing on a strong, battle-tested foundation. You’ll provide hands-on technical direction, lead a handpicked squad of engineers, and collaborate with senior stakeholders across Sales, Vendor Management, Finance, and the C-suite.

Key Responsibilities

    Lead Architecture & Technical Vision: Re-architect our Quote Engine into a next-generation, scalable, cloud-native platform using modern design patterns and technologies.
    Build & Lead a Team: Recruit, mentor, and manage a high-performance engineering team focused on reliability, scalability, and innovation.
    Collaborate Across the Org: Partner closely with cross-functional leaders in Sales, Finance, Vendor Management, and executive leadership to align technical initiatives with business goals.
    Ensure Operational Excellence: Drive high standards for performance, resilience, observability, and maintainability. Establish SLAs/SLOs and lead chaos testing and incident response practices.
    Champion Engineering Culture: Define best practices, write Engineering Playbooks, facilitate Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), and coach mid-level engineers on advanced systems concepts.

Must-Have Technical Expertise

    7+ years designing and writing production code in at least one of the following: Go, PHP, Python (asyncio)
    Proven experience with highly scalable, concurrent systems
    Expertise in:
        Multithreaded architectures and concurrency primitives (locks, CAS, futures, non-blocking I/O)
        Distributed systems design, including CAP trade-offs and consistency models
        Reactive/actor frameworks like Akka, Orleans, or Azure Service Fabric Reliable Actors
        Event-driven architectures and queue-centered microservices
        Designing idempotent, durable flows with Kafka, RabbitMQ, NATS, or Service Bus
        Back-pressure strategies, circuit breakers, and fault tolerance
        Azure (preferred), including Service Bus, Cosmos DB, Key Vault, and Azure Monitor
        Serverless orchestration with fan-out/fan-in strategies
        Authentication & Security: OAuth 2.0, HMAC, mTLS, secrets management, rate limiting

Systems & DevOps Familiarity

    Observability: OpenTelemetry, Grafana, Azure Monitor
    CI/CD: Canary releases, green/blue deployments
    Operational Readiness: Chaos drills, SLO/SLA planning, load-shedding
    Documentation: Engineering playbooks, technical ADRs
